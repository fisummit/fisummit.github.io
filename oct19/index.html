  Featuring  

Grant Sabatier is the creator of Millennial Money and author of Financial Freedom. Just after college he was unemployed, living at home with his parents, and had only $2.26 left in his bank account. 5 years later he had a net worth of $1 Million and has now help over tens of millions of readers learn about how to save more money, start a side hustle, make more money, invest intelligently, fast-track financial independence and reach early retirement.

JL Collins has been sharing his wealth of knowledge through his blog jlcollinsnh.com and is the author of The Simple Path to Wealth. JL Collins spent most of his career in the publishing business, but that is almost completely unimportant. What mattered (to him and now us) was his driving ambition to have F-you Money. Of all the things you can spend money on the most important, he says, is your freedom.

Elizabeth Willard Thames, better known as Mrs. Frugalwoods, is the creator of the award-winning personal finance blog, Frugalwoods.com and author of the book, Meet The Frugalwoods: Achieving Financial Independence Through Simple Living. At age 32, she reached financial independence and left a successful career in Boston to create a more meaningful, purpose-driven life on a 66-acre homestead in the woods of Vermont with her husband and their two young daughters. Thames has been featured on The Today Show, PBS, NPR, in the New York Times, Forbes, Parents Magazine, and more.

Nicole Hatcher is creator of the Frugal Chic Life blog and YouTube channel. She does it all, from money coach, wife, mother of three, makeup enthusiast, and lover of all things personal finance. Nicole paid off $90,000 of student loan debt in just 5 years and is on a mission to making sure other people can do the same.

Chad (Coach) Carson is a renowned real estate investor, creator of coachcarson.com and author of Retire Early with Real Estate. Chad was a linebacker at Clemson University and became an entrepreneur in 2003 right out of college with $1,000 in the bank. He had to hustle every day just to put food on the table and quickly learned the ins and outs of real estate investing to retire early and travel the world.

Jamila Souffrant, Certified Financial Education Instructor (CFEI), is the creator of the blog and podcast, Journey To Launch, named one of “27 Podcasts You Need To Start Listening To In 2018″ by BuzzFeed. She was raised by a single mom, is now married with three small children and has her family on track to becoming financially independent by the age of 40. She is the resident financial expert on News12, the most watched local TV news-station in NYC and has been featured in Money Magazine, TIME, Business Insider, Refinery29, CNBC, ESSENCE Magazine & CBS.

DAY 1
FREEDOM & DEBT

How to Stop Selling Out For Money and Start Doing More of What Matters

7:00pm ET | Chad (Coach) Carson

Money is simultaneously one of the most important and awkward topics in our life. Money alone can't make you happy, yet lack of money can make you miserable. In this session, you'll learn practical tips to find the "golden mean of money" where you use money as a powerful tool do things that matter like spending time with people you love, traveling, starting fun businesses, and making a difference in the world. You'll also learn how to figure out your financial independence number, how many rentals you need to retire (if real estate is your thing), and how to enjoy both the peak AND the plateaus during your climb to financial independence.

How to Achieve Debt Freedom and Make Room for Wealth

8:00pm ET | Nicole Hatcher - Frugal Chic Life

Making a commitment to tackle debt head-on is one of the first steps towards financial independence, but it’s only the tip of the iceberg. During this session, we’ll discuss how to craft a strategic plan to pay off debt so that you can work less and live more. We’ll also chat about money mindset and how to make the mental transition from drowning in debt to focusing on wealth.

DAY 2
BUDGET & INVEST

Investing Towards Your First $1 Million

7:00pm ET | JL Collins - Simple Path to Wealth

So you're starting to save more money than you ever have before but have no clue where to put it? In this session we'll discuss different investment strategies and portfolios to prepare for Financial Independence and how achievable your first $1 million really is. Discover the beauty of compund interest and how your hard earned money can produce passive income earlier than a traditional retirement age.

Creating Your Freedom Plan (a.k.a. a Budget)

8:00pm ET | Jamila Souffrant - Journey To Launch

When it comes to your goals of getting out of debt , saving and investing more money and ultimately reaching Financial Independence, you need a everyday plan for your money. In this session, I’ll show you the key steps in creating and sticking to a budget, your Freedom Plan. I’ll show you how to create a budget based on what you value and one that allows you to spend where it matters to you the most.

DAY 3
EARN & RETIRE

Optimizing Your Income: More Money in Less Time

7:00pm ET | Grant Sabatier - Millennial Money

We live in such an incredible time, where money making opportunities are literally everywhere. While it's not easy, it's definitely never been easier in history to make money and reach financial independence. Every $1,000 in consistent recurring income you can earn each month reduces your FI number by $300,000. In this session, Grant will not only show you the math behind the power of recurring income, but he'll share 5 different areas in your life where you can earn more money today and throughout your FI journey. He'll cover optimizing your full time job, why skills are future currency, how to pick, launch, and grow a profitable side hustle, and why making money isn't actually about the value you create in the world.

Creative Spending that Doesn't Sacrifice Your Retirement Dreams

8:00pm ET | Liz Thames - Frugalwoods

Yeah, yeah, yeah, we've all heard we need to spend less and save more in order to reach FI sooner, but how do you actually do that without abject deprivation? I'm no miser, I drink my fair share of craft beer, I live the luxuriously frugal life. I'm bringing frugal back and we'll talk all things frugality (and probably homesteading and parenting too since I do a lot of those in addition to thinking about ways to make frugality sound attractive). I'll take you through the idea and execution of uber frugal living so that you too can be a frugal weirdo (oh yeah! and retire early!).
